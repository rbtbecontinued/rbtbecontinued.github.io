About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the School of Electrical Engineering and Telecommunications, UNSW Sydney. My professional background spans both academia and industry. Prior to my PhD, I worked as a Researcher at the Radar and Electronic Equipment Research Institute, AVIC, Beijing, and as an Algorithm Engineer in the Ride-hailing Business Unit of Meituan, Beijing.

With a solid background in signal processing and machine learning, I have applied my expertise to a range of engineering problems. My recent research focuses on machine learning, (speech) signal processing, and sequence modeling.

Research

Integrated Speech Annotation and Analysis Framework

  • Develop advanced models and algorithms to support diverse speech processing tasks.
  • Build a model-assisted annotation workflow to improve efficiency of large-scale speech data annotation.
  • Develop an integrated platform that unifies model-assisted annotation, data management, and analytical tools.
Voice Activity Detection Speech Diarization Speech Recognition

Non-Autoregressive Generative Sequence Modeling

  • Analyze generative capabilities of diffusion models for time series generation from time and frequency perspectives.
  • Develop conditioned diffusion models for time series forecasting (e.g., energy usage forecasting).
Diffusion Model Time Series Analysis

Biologically-Inspired Approaches to Speech Recognition

  • Explore modeling techniques of biological mechanisms essential for human speech recognition.
  • Incorporate into existing speech recognition systems.
  • Bridge the gap between artificial technologies and human cognitive capabilities.
Speech Recognition Bayesian Deep Learning
